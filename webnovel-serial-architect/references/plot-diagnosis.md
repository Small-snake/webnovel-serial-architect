# Plot Diagnosis

Use this reference when the user is stuck, dissatisfied, or unsure why the story is not moving.

## Fast Diagnosis

Check these failure points:

- **No active goal**: The protagonist has no immediate objective.
- **Weak resistance**: The conflict is too easy or too abstract.
- **Delayed payoff**: The story promises satisfaction but withholds too long.
- **Setup overload**: Worldbuilding or backstory stops motion.
- **Character drift**: A character acts without pressure, desire, or cost.
- **Unclear hook**: The reader has no reason to open the next chapter.
- **Too many open loops**: Foreshadowing creates confusion instead of curiosity.
- **No escalation path**: The premise solves itself too early.

## Repair Moves

Offer specific fixes:

- Give the protagonist a near-term task with a visible consequence.
- Turn exposition into conflict, negotiation, pursuit, test, or reveal.
- Add a cost to the protagonist's advantage.
- Convert a passive scene into a choice.
- Pay off one small promise before planting three new ones.
- Merge redundant characters or assign each a distinct function.
- Move a later confrontation earlier if the opening lacks force.
- Split a large arc into a 5-10 chapter objective.

## Response Shape

Use this format:

```markdown
## Diagnosis

## Most Likely Root Cause

## Three Repair Options

## Recommended Next Step

## Next Chapter / Scene Patch
```
