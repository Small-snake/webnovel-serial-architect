# Market Positioning

Use this reference when the user needs genre, target reader, promise, market fit, or long-form viability.

## Positioning Questions

Answer these without forcing the user into a complete outline:

- What genre shelf does this most naturally sit on?
- What reader desire does it promise first?
- What is the main emotional engine:爽,甜,虐,燃,悬疑,成长,逆袭,救赎,权谋,探索?
- What makes the protagonist worth following for hundreds of chapters?
- What can keep escalating after the first arc?
- What is familiar enough for readers to enter quickly?
- What is different enough to avoid feeling generic?

## Genre Fit

Map the idea to one primary lane and one optional secondary lane:

- Urban, system, rebirth, transmigration, fantasy, xianxia, historical, military, suspense, romance, campus, workplace, entertainment industry, apocalypse, infinite flow, game, farming, family, revenge.

Do not over-blend genres early. If the idea contains many elements, pick the element that drives the opening conflict.

## Reader Promise

State the promise in this format:

```text
This story is for readers who want [core feeling] through [main mechanism], with [distinctive hook].
```

Examples of mechanisms:

- weak-to-strong growth
- status reversal
- hidden identity reveal
- emotional pursuit and avoidance
- mystery solving
- survival pressure
- business expansion
- cultivation breakthrough
- revenge chain
- relationship repair

## Long-Form Viability Check

Rate the idea on:

- **Escalation space**: Can conflict grow by stages?
- **Repeatable payoff**: Can the reader receive satisfying beats often?
- **Character pressure**: Does the protagonist have goals that generate choices?
- **Antagonistic force**: Is there durable resistance beyond one villain?
- **World expansion**: Can the story open new circles, maps, families, industries, sects, cases, missions, or power levels?

If one area is weak, propose a patch rather than rejecting the premise.
