# Inspiration System

Use this reference when the user has fragments rather than a complete story.

## Goal

Turn scattered creative material into a living bank that can feed outlines, openings, characters, scenes, and later twists.

## Capture Categories

- **Premise spark**: a one-line "what if", reversal, setting, relationship, or power fantasy.
- **Character spark**: identity, wound, desire, contradiction, skill, flaw, social position, secret.
- **Conflict spark**: pressure, enemy, taboo, debt, mission, misunderstanding, impossible choice.
- **World spark**: rule, hierarchy, city, sect, company, family, system, magic, technology, social custom.
- **Power spark**: system, golden finger, cheat, profession, talent, resource, hidden knowledge.
- **Payoff spark**: face-slapping, rescue, reveal, promotion, revenge, romance beat, mystery answer.
- **Scene image**: a vivid moment, gesture, location, object, entrance, confrontation, or ending hook.
- **Dialogue or line**: a memorable phrase, threat, promise, joke, confession, or slogan.
- **Description material**: appearance, action, atmosphere, sensory detail, object, clothing, weapon, room.
- **Market observation**: hot trope, reader desire, comment-section pain point, platform trend, title pattern.
- **Foreshadowing seed**: object, rumor, unfinished sentence, strange rule, old debt, hidden identity.

## Processing Steps

1. Preserve the user's raw note before reshaping it.
2. Tag the note by category, genre fit, emotional payoff, and likely story position.
3. Decide whether it should become a premise, character, conflict, setting, scene, hook, or deferred material.
4. If it is strong, expand it into 2-3 possible uses.
5. If it is weak but interesting, store it as tentative material instead of discarding it.

## Inspiration Card Format

Use this compact structure:

```markdown
## Inspiration Card

- Raw note:
- Category:
- Genre fit:
- Reader payoff:
- Possible story position:
- Expansion option A:
- Expansion option B:
- Keep / merge / defer / discard:
```

## Daily Material Practice

When the user wants writing practice rather than outline work, offer small drills:

- Convert a scene image into 150-300 words of atmosphere.
- Rewrite an action beat with clearer cause and effect.
- Create three appearance details that reveal status or personality.
- Turn a market trope into a more specific protagonist pressure.
- Take a line of dialogue and design the conflict that would make it hurt.
