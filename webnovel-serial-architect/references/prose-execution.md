# Prose Execution

Use this reference when the user asks to draft, rewrite, continue, or diagnose actual chapter prose.

## Principle

Write prose that serves the serial promise already chosen. Do not flatten the user's voice into generic webnovel narration. Preserve viewpoint, tension, and the story's current level of uncertainty.

## Before Drafting

Check or ask for these only when missing:

- Viewpoint: first person, close third, omniscient, rotating, or deliberately unstable.
- Scene task: what must change by the end of the scene.
- Reader question: what should the reader want answered next.
- Information boundary: what the viewpoint character knows, suspects, hides, or misunderstands.
- Payoff: what small satisfaction, reversal, dread, clue, or emotional beat appears in this scene.

## Draft Output Shape

For new prose, provide:

```markdown
## Draft Intent

## Chapter / Scene Draft

## Hook And Continuation Note
```

For revision, provide:

```markdown
## Diagnosis

## Revision Strategy

## Revised Version

## What Changed
```

## First-Chapter Execution

For a first chapter, prioritize:

- Immediate pressure over background explanation.
- A protagonist action or refusal within the opening.
- Concrete sensory detail that implies genre and mood.
- One clear contradiction: dream/reality, identity/behavior, memory/evidence, promise/cost.
- A chapter ending that opens a specific next question.

## Suspense And Unreliable Narration

For dreamlike, mystery, hospital, dual-world, or psychologically unstable premises:

- Keep evidence graded: witnessed, remembered, recorded, inferred, denied by others.
- Do not explain the full mechanism too early.
- Let the prose create two plausible readings when useful: psychological symptom and external reality.
- Anchor surreal details in physical consequences so ambiguity has weight.
- Use recurring objects, symptoms, time gaps, written records, or bodily traces as continuity hooks.

## Style Controls

When revising prose, tune one or two dimensions at a time:

- **Tension**: sharpen goal, threat, deadline, or cost.
- **Density**: remove explanation that does not alter the current scene.
- **Viewpoint**: keep perception inside the chosen narrator's knowledge boundary.
- **Rhythm**: vary sentence length around reveals, actions, and internal reaction.
- **Hook**: end on a decision, discovery, reversal, arrival, or withheld answer.

## Guardrails

- Do not generate a full long chapter unless the user asks for length.
- When offering variants, make each variant structurally different, not just reworded.
- Label invented details as optional when they are not grounded in the user's material.
- If existing drafts are present, align names, rules, timeline, and tone before writing continuation.
